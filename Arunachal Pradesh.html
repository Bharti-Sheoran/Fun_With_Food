Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal pradesh "The land of rising sun" is one of the largest among North eastern states.Arunachal Pradesh has among the highest diversity of mammals and birds in India. Agriculture primarily drives the economy.Arunachal Pradesh can be roughly divided into a set of semi-distinct cultural spheres, on the basis of tribal identity, language, religion and material culture: the Tibetic-speaking Monpa area bordering Bhutan in the west, the Tani area in the centre of the state, the Mishmi area to the east of the Tani area, the Tai/Singpho/Tangsa area bordering Myanmar, and the Naga area to the south, which also borders Myanmar.

Bamboo Shoots Cutlet

Bamboo Shoot is very commonly consumed in North Eastern part of India and many other Asian countries. It is low in calories and fat, but high in fiber. It also contains vitamins and minerals and is good for the diabetics. It can be cooked with / without veggies or with any type of meat.

Ingredients

  • 200 gms. bamboo shoot
  • 1 potato, boiled & grated / mashed
  • 2-3 tbsp. roasted chickpea flour
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tsp. ginger, grated
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped
  • 2-3 tbsp. coriander leaves, chopped
  • to taste salt
  • 1/4 tsp. turmeric powder
  • As required extra chickpea flour for dusting
  • 1 tsp. dry roasted coriander-cumin powder
  • 2-3 tbsp. oil to shallow fry
  • 1/2 tsp. garam masala powder
  • 1 tsp. flaxseed (opt)

How to make Bamboo Shoots Cutlet

  • Boil the bamboo shoot with a pinch of turmeric powder. Grate or process them and keep aside.
  • Mix all the ingredients, except oil and divide into equal portions.
  • Give a round shape or any other shape of your preference to them and refrigerate for a couple of minute.
  • Heat some oil in a pan. Dust the cutlets with some chickpea flour & shallow fry them in batches till golden brown on both sides. Drain on a kitchen towel.
  • Relish with some chutney or sauce.

Zan Recipe - Finger Millet Porridge with Vegetables Recipe

Zan is the most popular porridge recipe of monpa tribes of arunachal pradesh.It is prepared using millet flour.They have this as breakfast before going to work. It is nutritious and keeps you full.

Ingredients

  • 4 tablespoons Ragi Flour (Finger Millet/ Nagli)
  • 1 1/2 cup Water
  • 1 Carrot (Gajjar) , sliced diagonally
  • 1 cup Spinach
  • 1/4 cup Soybeans (Whole Soya dal) , boiled
  • 1/4 cup Green peas (Matar) , optional
  • Salt , as per taste
  • 1 teaspoon Black pepper powder
  • 1 sprig Coriander (Dhania) Leaves , chopped
  • 1 tablespoon Ghee

How to make Arunachal Pradesh Zan Recipe

  • We begin making the Arunachal Pradesh Zan Recipe (Finger Millet Porridge with Vegetables Recipe) roast the millet flour with a tablespoon of ghee in a kadai and set aside.
  • Dissolve millet flour in little water and cook until it becomes semi thick paste.
  • Saute the vegetable separately in a flat tawa with little oil, saute the carrot first so that it get cooked and then add in the spinach. Keep it aside.
  • You can also blanch the vegetables in water with salt till it becomes tender and then add it to the ragi porridge.
  • Take about one and half cup of water in vessel and bring it to a boil. Add this millet flour paste and mix it.
  • At this point add the vegetables and seasoning like salt, pepper and herbs. Keep whisking it till you get your desired consistency.
  • Serve it hot with by sprinkling some fermented cheese or just plain as well.
  • Serve the Arunachal Pradesh Zan Recipe (Finger Millet Porridge with Vegetables Recipe) as a healthy breakfast with Cantaloupe (Melon) Juice Recipe to have a satisfying breakfast.


Khura | Buckwheat Pancake

Arunachal Pradesh BreakfastBuckwheat Pancakes, a traditional Monpa tribe breakfast, served with butter tea made of Yak milk.

Prep time: 5 mins | Rest time: 1 Hr | Cook time: 15 mins | Total time: ~1 hr 15 mins

Ingredients

  • 1 Cup Buckwheat flour
  • Water to mix (1)
  • Salt to taste
  • Butter for cooking

How to make Khura

  • Mix flour and water in a bowl. Let the batter be slightly thick. Let it stand for an hour.
  • Heat a griddle or tawa in a medium flame. Smear some butter and spread the batter. You cannot spread it thin like a dosa. So try as much as you could without it getting stuck. These will be slightly thicker.
  • Once the bottom sode browns, flip and cook on the other side.

Khapse

Made popularly during the Tibetan New Year, or Losar, Khapse is a variety of sweet biscuits made from flour, butter, eggs and sugar. Its deep fried and served in different shapes, sizes and twists.

Total Time30mPrep Time10 mCalories404

Ingredients

  • 4 cup all purpose flour
  • 6 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 cup boiling water
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup refined oil

How to make Khapse

  • Begin with dissolving sugar in warm water in a small bowl. Take a deep bowl and add all purpose flour, milk and refined oil in it, followed by the prepared water-sugar mixture. Mix all the ingredients well.
  • Now, knead the content of the bowl in a tender and thick dough of 1/4-inches. Slice the dough into long vertical shapes.
  • Mark a tender cut in the middle of the vertical dough piece and gently twist the ends of the dough to get a circular pattern. Repeat the same step with the remaining dough.
  • Take a deep-bottomed pan or a kadhai, keep it on medium flame and heat refined oil in it. Once the oil is heated, add the prepared khapse in the oil and deep fry them until golden brown in colour. Take them out on a absorbing paper and serve immediately.
