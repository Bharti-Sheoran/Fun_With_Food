Chhattisgarh

Chhattisgarh is one of the fastest-developing states in India.The official languages of the state are Chhattisgarhi and Hindi. The state hosts many religious sects such as Satnampanth, Kabirpanth, Ramnami Samaj and othersThe State of Chhattisgarh is known as the rice bowl of India and has a rich tradition of food culture.The typical Chhattisgarhi thali consists of roti, bhat, dal or kadhi, curry, chutney and bhaji. Few Chhattisgarhi dishes are Aamat, Bafauri, Bhajia, Chousela, Dubkikadhi, Farra, Khurmi, Moong Bara, Thethari, and Muthia.

Dehrori

Made primarily from rice and molten sugar syrup, Dehrori is known as one of the most famous sweet-dishes of the state.Dehrori is a scrumptious Bihari dessert that owes its origin to Chattisgarh but is popular worldwide with different names.

Total Time: 50min \Prep Time: 20 min \Calories: 663

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice
  • 2 tablespoon almonds
  • 4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup yoghurt (curd)
  • water as required
  • 1/2 teaspoon powdered green cardamom
  • 2 tablespoon cashews
  • 2 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 3 cup ghee

How to make Dehrori

  • Take a bowl and add water and rice in it. Soak the rice in the water for at least 5 to 6 hours. Once done, remove the water from the bowl and retain the rice. Transfer this rice into a mixer jar and grind it into a raw paste. Stir in curd into the rice paste and leave overnight to ferment.
  • In a deep bottomed pan kept on medium flame, add water and sugar. Heat the mixture until you get a syrup like consistency. Check whether you get a string like texture once you dip a spoon in the mixture. If you do, turn off the flame. Add cardamom powder and lime juice in the sugar syrup and mix.
  • Take a deep bottomed pan, keep it on medium flame and heat ghee in it. Meanwhile make small pan cakes out of the rice-curd mixture. Gently dip these pan cakes in the hot ghee and fry them until golden brown in color.
  • Immediately transfer the fried pan cakes in the sugar syrup and let it soak the syrup. Once done, garnish the patties with chopped almonds and cashews. Serve!

Fara

Fara is an authentic Chhattisgarh snack, which is easy to make and less time-consuming. It is rich in energy and also consider as a glutenfree recipe. Fara is mainly made with rice and rice flour. It is best to eat with green chutney or homemade pickel.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice flour
  • to taste Salt
  • 1 spoon Musterd seeds
  • leaves Curry 8-10
  • 1/2 cup Oil

How to make Fara

  • Add 1 small spoon of oil and add salt
  • Add water to make a little hard dough
  • Grease both palm with oil make small size shape like fingers

  • When water boiled add finger shapes in it
  • Cover the pan and boil in ful flame
  • After 5 minuts stir and cook in medium flame
  • When the water finished cook in low flmae
  • Check the fara with knife when it comes clean fara is ready to eat
  • Serve hot with chutney or sauce

Zimikhand

The mouthwatering Zimikhand curry or khadi is one of the favorite dish of people from Chhattisgarh. Zimikhand is also known as rataloo or sooran in the part of India.

Ingredients

  • Zimikhand
  • oil
  • mustard seed
  • kadi leaves
  • 1-2 tomato
  • salt, turmeric powder, coriander powder
  • curd
  • gram flour
  • water

How to make Zimikhand

  • Boil Zimikhand with alum(fitkari) for 10-15 min.
  • Peelout the outer brown layer once it is cold and cut into rectangular pieces.Dry them on the sun for some time.
  • Once it is dried, take a pan and hit the oil and deep fry those pieces. Take 3-4 tablespoon oil and heat in a different pan.
  • Add mustard seeds, curry leaves, chopped tomato and keep frying until it turns red. Add salt, turmeric powder, and coriander powder and let it cook for some more time in the slow flame.
  • Now add fried pieces of Zimikhand in the cooked mixture and cook for some more time.
  • Take 500gm curd and mix 1-2 tablespoon gram flour and mix some water to make this curd little thinner.
  • Add the curd in cooked Zimikhand and let the whole mix cook some more time until curd gets a boil.
  • After 5-7 min turns off the gas and lets Zimikhand kadi get cold. Enjoy this kadi with steamed rice.
